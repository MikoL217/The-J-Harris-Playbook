1
REINVENT THROUGH PURPOSE
The Principle:

Great leaders reinvent themselves through purpose and impact, not comfort.

Harris's Story:

Started 26North at 55 after financial success to maximize positive impact using his "secret sauce" investing and building excellent organizations.

Application for Your Team:

  • What would you do if financial success was guaranteed? That's your purpose
  • Late career doesn't mean final chapter—it means experience + wisdom + resources
  • Ask: "What impact do I want to have?" not "What more can I achieve?"
Action Step: Schedule quarterly "purpose check-ins" to ensure work aligns with impact goals
2
EVOLVE FROM DOER TO LEADER
The Principle:

Sustainable success requires shifting from control to influence.

The Three Stages:

  • DOER: Master the craft, execute deals, do the work
  • MANAGER: Build systems, oversee teams, delegate execution
  • LEADER: Shape culture, vision, values—create environment where excellence emerges

Harris's Evolution:

Started as dealmaker at Apollo, evolved to building cultures at 26North.

Application for Your Team:

  • Identify which stage you're in (most leaders get stuck at Manager)
  • Leader stage = asking "What does our culture stand for?"
  • Culture scales results faster than any strategic plan
Action Step: List 3 decisions you made this week. Which stage was each from? Shift more to Leader.
3
PRACTICE SERVANT LEADERSHIP
The Principle:

Empowering others' success creates exponential impact beyond individual capacity.

Harris's Approach:

  • "What drives me is impacting other people positively"
  • To team: "I'll help you achieve personal financial success"
  • From team: "Your job is to help us win"

The Multiplier Effect:

  • Servant leaders develop leaders, not followers
  • Empowerment breeds ownership and accountability
  • People perform at highest levels when leader invests in their growth

Application for Your Team:

  • Replace "How can they help me?" with "How can I help them succeed?"
  • Measure your success by your team's growth, not just results
  • Make others' development a KPI, not just a nice-to-have
Action Step: This week, ask each direct report: "What's one thing I can do to help you succeed?"
4
ALIGN INCENTIVES WITH OUTCOMES
The Principle:

What you pay for is what you get—compensation reveals organizational values.

26North's Model:

  • Talent takes less cash, bets on entrepreneurial upside
  • Compensation tied to alpha generation, not management fees
  • Individuals/teams do better financially but must align collectively
  • "If we don't align, we all lose"

Why It Works:

  • Public firms optimize for predictable management fees
  • Private structure allows pure focus on performance/returns
  • Ownership mentality produces higher creativity and accountability

Application for Your Team:

  • Audit current incentives: Do they reward activity or outcomes?
  • Ensure team incentives align with organizational goals
  • Make sure individual success requires collective success
Action Step: Review your team's incentives this quarter. Do they drive the behavior you want?
5
HIRE FOR RESILIENCE
The Principle:

When everyone is smart, grit and adversity navigation separate winners.

Harris's Hiring Criteria:

  1. Excellence in craft
  2. Mission-driven mindset
  3. Positive impact orientation
  4. "Willing to bring it" (intensity/commitment)

The Adversity Advantage:

  • "People who've gone through challenge early in life generally do better"
  • Wrestling taught Harris: physical/mental toughness, picking yourself up after losses
  • Resilience = ability to face setbacks, refocus on positives, get back in arena

Cultural Fit > Raw Talent:

  • Turned down highly competent people wrong for community
  • "Smart people exist on both sides of you—ability to dig through tough situations sets you apart"

Application for Your Team:

  • Interview for resilience stories, not just accomplishments
  • Ask: "Tell me about a time you failed and what you did next"
  • Hire people you want to be around, not just people good at jobs
Action Step: Add "resilience assessment" to your next hiring process
6
MANAGE YOURSELF OUT OF EVERY JOB
The Principle:

Build systems and develop people so organization thrives without constant involvement.

Harris's Approach:

  • At 26North: CEO + Investment Committee Chair, but increasingly develops others
  • Sports: Hire great GM, coach, CEO/president—then delegate
  • "In every role, I've tried to manage myself out of the job"

Why It Matters:

  • Only way to scale across $30B asset manager + largest sports portfolio
  • Leaders who can't let go create organizational bottlenecks
  • Building leadership capacity in others is true leadership

Application for Your Team:

  • For each responsibility, ask: "Who could do this in 6 months?"
  • Develop successors for your current role
  • Your goal: make yourself unnecessary in current capacity
Action Step: Identify one responsibility to delegate this month and begin training your replacement
7
PROTECT YOUR FOCUS
The Principle:

Focus is a leader's scarcest resource—design calendar around mission, not inbox.

Harris's Priority Hierarchy:

  1. Health: "If you're not healthy, you're not good to anyone"
  2. Family: "Raising great human beings is how you impact the world"
  3. Business: Through intentional time management

The Three Intentional Questions:

  • Who do I put around me?
  • Which decisions require my involvement?
  • What issues do I focus on?

Master issue-spotting and delegation to scale effectively.

Application for Your Team:

  • Block calendar for health/family FIRST, then business
  • Audit last week: how much time on strategic vs. tactical?
  • Protect time for strategic thinking—it's your highest value activity
Action Step: Block 2 hours this week for "strategic thinking only"—no meetings, no email
8
BUILD COMMUNITY, NOT JUST TEAMS
The Principle:

Emotional connection compounds brand equity faster than financial engineering.

Sports as Model:

  • Commanders' NFC Championship: entire DC area celebrated regardless of economics, race, religion
  • "Sports is a community" in a world where internet/COVID broke down connection
  • Creates belonging in divided, polarized times

Business Application:

  • People want to belong to something meaningful
  • Community creates loyalty that transactions can't buy
  • Mission-driven organizations generate emotional equity = sustainable advantage

Harris's Standard:

  • Hire people who are competent AND people want to be around
  • "We're not for everyone—you've got to really want to be part of this"

Application for Your Team:

  • Do team members feel they belong to community or just work at company?
  • Create shared experiences beyond work tasks
  • Build culture where people choose to be, not just paid to be
Action Step: Create one "community-building" activity this month (not team-building—community-building)
9
PRACTICE STEWARDSHIP OVER OWNERSHIP
The Principle:

Long-term value comes from doing right by all stakeholders.

Harris's Sports Philosophy:

  • "I consider teams being owned by the city, I'm a steward for the cities"
  • Invests capital, takes risk, but fundamental role is stewardship
  • "If you do what's right for the city, you end up in a good place"

Commanders Stadium Example:

  • $2.7B investment (largest in Washington history)
  • Four neighborhoods, affordable housing, job creation, massive ROI for city
  • Community support was explosive when vision aligned with their values

Application for Your Team:

  • Replace "What can I extract?" with "What can I contribute?"
  • Stakeholder value > shareholder value in long run
  • Stewardship mindset changes decision time horizons
Action Step: List your key stakeholders. Rate how well you're serving each. Pick one to improve this quarter.
10
INVEST IN THE UNDERVALUED
The Principle:

First-mover advantage in undervalued markets creates asymmetric returns.

Harris's Women's Sports Bet:

  • "We're at an existential moment in women's sports"
  • WNBA viewership at 60% of NBA levels (Caitlin Clark effect)
  • Economic opportunity + equity responsibility
  • Investing in flag football (Olympic sport, 50%+ female participation)

The Role Model Effect:

  • Wrestling role models "changed my life"
  • "We need to create that for girls and for women"
  • Role models create generational talent pipelines

Application for Your Team:

  • Where are the undervalued opportunities in your market?
  • What segments are growing but underinvested?
  • Social responsibility + business opportunity often align
Action Step: Identify one undervalued opportunity in your business and create 90-day exploration plan
11
COMPOUND EARLY, HARVEST LATER
The Principle:

The 15-30 age window sets lifetime trajectory through intentional narrative-building.

Harris's Advice:

  • "When you're 15-30, you're building your narrative"
  • "If you want to play hard, make sure you work hard too"
  • "If you do good job in those 15 years, the ROI's for next 50 years"

Career Building Blocks:

  • Trust your gut: Follow what genuinely energizes you
  • Seek challenge: Go for smartest people where you'll be most challenged
  • Volume + focus: Excellence requires lots of practice in focused area
  • Choose partners wisely: Surround yourself with people you like and respect

Application for Your Team:

  • Mentor younger team members on long-term thinking
  • Share your own narrative-building stories
  • Help them see connection between current work and future opportunity
Action Step: Mentor one emerging leader this quarter on their 15-year career narrative
12
WORK FROM FULFILLMENT
The Principle:

Work aligned with purpose creates energy; fulfillment makes you better in all life domains.

Harris's Controversial Truth:

  • Lifetime of hearing: "You work too hard. Why do this?"
  • Response: "Because I want to. I like to work hard."
  • "I'm better father, husband, person because I'm fulfilled when building something"

The Fulfillment Paradox:

  • Work creates energy when aligned with purpose (versus depleting it)
  • Being fulfilled at work makes you better at home
  • People who love what they do don't need to be motivated

Self-Selection Philosophy:

  • "We're not for everyone. You've got to really want to do this"
  • Athletes put "heart and soul" into it—same required for 26North
  • "If you want that, great. Come be part of it if you've got the goods"

Application for Your Team:

  • Don't apologize for high standards or intensity
  • Help people find work that fulfills them (even if it's elsewhere)
  • Build culture where commitment is celebrated, not questioned
Action Step: Assess your own fulfillment (1-10). If below 7, identify what needs to change.

THE HARRIS WINNING FORMULA

THE FOUNDATION

"Win with the best team on the field by having the best team off the field."

Excellence in visible performance requires excellence in invisible preparation. Culture determines capability.

THE VALUE EXCHANGE

  • Leader to Team: "I'll help you achieve personal financial success"
  • Team to Leader: "Your job is to help us win"
  • Reality: Neither succeeds without the other—alignment creates mutual accountability

WINNING DEFINED

In Sports:

  • Winning titles
  • Creating fan happiness

In Business:

  • EBITDA, cash flow, stock price
  • Stakeholder value creation

Universal Truth:

  • Excellence requires obsessive commitment
  • Not for everyone—requires explicit desire
  • Must "bring it" every day with heart and soul

  • Are incentives aligned with desired outcomes?
  • Do people belong to community or just work here?

Organizational Assessment:

  • Are we building for next quarter or next decade?

  6. Protect Focus: Health + family + strategic thinking
  7. Create Community: Belonging > transactions
  8. Practice Stewardship: Long-term > short-term
  9. Find Undervalued: See what others miss
  10. Compound Early: 15-30 sets 50-year trajectory
  11. Work From Joy: Fulfillment fuels excellence
  12. Demand Excellence: High standards, no apologies
